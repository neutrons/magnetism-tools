α-RuCl3 — Zigzag order and the role of the k-vector#

α-RuCl3 adopts the R\(\bar{3}\) structure (No. 148) at low temperature and orders in a zigzag pattern with propagation vector k = (0, 1/2, 1) in the hexagonal setting. The Ru3+ ions occupy the 6c Wyckoff site.

This tutorial shows how the choice of k affects the symmetry analysis, producing a qualitatively different result from CrCl3 despite both materials sharing the same parent space group.

Setup#

import numpy as np
from msgjson import maximal_msgs, subgroup_msgs, analyze_msg

SG      = 148              # R-3
K       = [0, 0.5, 1.0]   # zigzag propagation vector (hexagonal setting)
RU_SITE = [0, 0, 1/3]     # Ru^3+ at 6c

The little co-group of k#

The first step is to identify which rotation matrices map k to ±**k** modulo the R-centered reciprocal lattice. For CrCl3 with k = (0, 0, 3/2) the entire S6 point group (all six rotations of R\(\bar{3}\)) was k-compatible. For α-RuCl3 the situation is different:

  • The C3 rotation maps (0, 1/2, 1) → (1/2, 0, 1), which is not equivalent to ±**k** modulo the R lattice.

  • Only the identity E and inversion i satisfy the k-compatibility condition.

The little co-group is therefore Ci = {E, i}, a strict subgroup of S6. This has direct consequences for moment symmetry.

Maximal MSGs#

top = maximal_msgs(SG, k=K, sites=[RU_SITE], centering="R")

for r in top:
    s = r.sites[0]
    print(
        f"BNS {r.bns_number}  type={r.msg_type}  parent_sg={r.parent_sg}"
        f"  n_ops={r.n_ops}  n_free={s.n_free}"
        f"  orbit={len(s.orbit)}  origin={r.origin_shift}"
    )

BNS 2.7  type=4  parent_sg=2  n_ops=4  n_free=3  orbit=4  origin=(0.0, 0.0, 0.0)
BNS 2.7  type=4  parent_sg=2  n_ops=4  n_free=3  orbit=4  origin=(0.0, 0.0, 0.5)

The k-maximal MSG is BNS 2.7 (\(P_S\bar{1}\), type IV), whose parent space group is SG 2 (P\(\bar{1}\)) — not the input parent SG 148 (R\(\bar{3}\)). This is the defining feature of the Bilbao MAXMAGN definition [1]: a MSG is k-maximal if it has no k-compatible supergroup anywhere in the subgroup lattice of G1’. Because the little co-group of k = (0, 1/2, 1) contains only W ∈ {E, i}, the entire R-3 operator set lies outside the little co-group. No MSG belonging to SG 148 can be k-compatible — the k-maximal MSG therefore crosses the parent-SG boundary to the P\(\bar{1}\) family.

The two results again represent inequivalent origin placements (domain states) of the type-IV anti-translation τ = (0, 0, 1/2).

n_free = 3 for both: with no C3 constraint anywhere in the P\(\bar{1}\) symmetry, all moment directions — including in-plane — are already allowed at the maximal level. This contrasts with CrCl3, where the k-maximal MSG (also BNS 148.20) imposed m ‖ c.

Subgroup lattice#

all_r = subgroup_msgs(SG, k=K, sites=[RU_SITE], centering="R")
print(f"{len(all_r)} candidate MSGs\n")

print(f"{'BNS':10s}  type  parent  n_ops  n_free")
for r in all_r:
    s = r.sites[0]
    print(
        f"  {r.bns_number:8s}    {r.msg_type}     {r.parent_sg:3d}"
        f"     {r.n_ops:2d}       {s.n_free}"
    )

Only 7 candidates compared to 27 for CrCl3. Because the little co-group contains only W ∈ {E, i}, the subgroup lattice is exhausted by the P\(\bar{1}\) (parent 2) and P1 (parent 1) families. All MSGs from SG 148 itself are absent — the R-3 rotations (C3, S6) are not k-compatible with k = (0, 1/2, 1) and so cannot appear as operators in any candidate MSG.

The key difference is not the parent space group but the propagation vector: k = (0, 0, 3/2) preserves the three-fold axis in the little co-group so the k-maximal MSG stays within the R-3 family; k = (0, 1/2, 1) expels C3 from the little co-group entirely, driving the k-maximal MSG to the P\(\bar{1}\) family and lifting the c-axis constraint from the outset.
