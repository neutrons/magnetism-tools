MnO — Type-II antiferromagnet on an FCC lattice#

MnO (manganese oxide) is a textbook type-II antiferromagnet with the rock-salt structure, space group \(Fm\bar{3}m\) (No. 225). The Mn2+ ions sit at the 4a Wyckoff site (0, 0, 0) and its face-centred equivalents. Below \(T_N \approx 118\) K they order with propagation vector k = (1/2, 1/2, 1/2), the L point of the FCC Brillouin zone, stacking ferromagnetic (111) planes antiferromagnetically.

Setup#

import numpy as np
from msgjson import maximal_msgs, subgroup_msgs

SG      = 225              # Fm-3m
K       = [0.5, 0.5, 0.5] # L point
MN_SITE = [0, 0, 0]        # Mn^2+ at 4a

The sublattice structure#

The propagation vector k = (1/2, 1/2, 1/2) assigns a phase \(e^{2\pi i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{r}}\) to each site. In the FCC conventional cell the four Mn positions split 1:3 between the two magnetic sublattices:

The (111) planes containing the “+” site alternate with those containing three “−” sites, giving the characteristic layered AFM pattern.

Maximal MSG#

top = maximal_msgs(SG, k=K, sites=[MN_SITE], centering="F")

for r in top:
    s = r.sites[0]
    print(
        f"BNS {r.bns_number}  type={r.msg_type}  parent_sg={r.parent_sg}"
        f"  n_ops={r.n_ops}  n_free={s.n_free}"
        f"  orbit={len(s.orbit)}  origin={r.origin_shift}"
    )

BNS 2.7  type=4  parent_sg=2  n_ops=4  n_free=3  orbit=2  origin=(0.0, 0.0, 0.5)

The result is BNS 2.7 (\(P_S\bar{1}\), parent SG 2), the same k-maximal MSG returned for α-RuCl3. This reflects a basis-mismatch limitation of the subgroup-lattice search (identical to the one documented in the subgroup section below): the true k-maximal MSGs for Fm\(\bar{3}\)m at k = (1/2, 1/2, 1/2) are the rhombohedral RI-3m and RI-3c families [1], but expressing their C3 rotation in the hexagonal basis gives a different W-matrix than C3[111] in the cubic basis. The W-matrix subset check cannot recognise them as equivalent, so only the basis-independent P\(\bar{1}\) / P1 families are found.

Bilbao’s MAXMAGN [1] gives four k-maximal MSGs for this case (two from the RI-3m branch and two from RI-3c, one per conjugacy class), of which only one allows a nonzero spin on the Mn atom at the origin. Once the correct MSG is identified by Bilbao or GSAS-II, analyze_msg() can compute the moment basis for any candidate.

Subgroup lattice search#

all_r = subgroup_msgs(SG, k=K, sites=[MN_SITE], centering="F")
print(f"{len(all_r)} candidate MSGs")
for r in all_r:
    s = r.sites[0]
    print(f"  BNS {r.bns_number:10s}  type={r.msg_type}  parent={r.parent_sg:3d}  n_free={s.n_free}")

Only 7 candidates, all in the P\(\bar{1}\) or P1 families.

The little co-group of k = (1/2, 1/2, 1/2) in \(Fm\bar{3}m\) is D3d — 12 unique rotation matrices including C3[111] expressed in the cubic basis as

\[\begin{split}W_{C_3} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}.\end{split}\]

The physically relevant subgroups of \(Fm\bar{3}m\) that allow in-plane moments — such as the rhombohedral R\(\bar{3}\) family along [111] — also contain a three-fold axis, but express it in the hexagonal basis:

\[\begin{split}W_{C_3}^{\text{hex}} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 1 & 0 \\ -1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}.\end{split}\]

These two matrices represent the same geometric rotation in different coordinate systems; the current W-matrix comparison in subgroup_msgs() does not perform the basis transformation needed to recognise them as equivalent. As a result, the trigonal/rhombohedral subgroups are absent from the search results, and only the trivially-basis-independent P\(\bar{1}\) and P1 families are recovered.

For MnO, the full subgroup analysis including the proper rhombohedral treatment is available through the Bilbao k-SUBGROUPSMAG tool [1] or directly in GSAS-II [2] and Jana2020 [4]. Once the MSG is identified by those tools, analyze_msg() can be used to compute the moment basis for any specific candidate.
